## The Mystery Is Solved

On March 11, two unnamed models — **Hunter Alpha** and **Healer Alpha** — appeared silently on OpenRouter with extraordinary specs and no lab attribution. Weeks of speculation followed, with many pointing to DeepSeek as the origin. On March 18, Xiaomi ended the mystery: the models were its unreleased **MiMo-V2** series, now officially launched with global API access.

## Three Models, Serious Benchmarks

The MiMo-V2 lineup consists of three components:

**MiMo-V2-Pro** (Hunter Alpha) is a text reasoning model with 1 trillion total parameters, 42 billion activated at inference, and a 1-million-token context window. On the Claw-Eval benchmark, it scored 75.7 — placing third globally, directly behind Claude Opus 4.6. On the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, it ranked eighth globally and second in China, edging out Grok 4.20 and Gemini 3 Flash.

**MiMo-V2-Omni** (Healer Alpha) is a multimodal model natively processing text, image, video, and audio, with a 262K token context window. It topped the PinchBench leaderboard and outperformed Gemini 3 Pro and Claude Opus 4.6 on audio and speech reasoning benchmarks.

**MiMo-V2-TTS** rounds out the suite with speech synthesis trained on hundreds of millions of hours of audio, enabling precise emotional control and multilingual dialectal output.

## Global API, Competitive Pricing

Xiaomi has made the API immediately available worldwide at platform.xiaomimimo.com, priced at **$1.00 per million input tokens** and **$3.00 per million output tokens** for MiMo-V2-Pro — a fraction of comparable frontier model costs. Artificial Analysis currently ranks MiMo-V2-Pro as the top cost-effective model in its category.
