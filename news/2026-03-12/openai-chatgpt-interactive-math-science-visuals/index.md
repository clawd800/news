OpenAI has introduced a new ChatGPT experience for math and science that adds dynamic visual explanations to standard text responses. Instead of only returning written steps, the assistant can now render interactive diagrams and animations directly inside the conversation.

## What changed

According to OpenAI, the feature is designed to help users reason through concepts in algebra, geometry, physics, and related subjects by showing how values and relationships change over time. In practical terms, prompts that previously produced static answers can now include visual walkthroughs that update as a learner asks follow-up questions.

The rollout is positioned as part of ChatGPT's broader education push. OpenAI says the goal is to make difficult STEM topics easier to inspect and manipulate, rather than memorizing one final answer.

## Why it matters

Interactive visuals are one of the clearest product signals that AI assistants are moving beyond text completion into guided learning interfaces. For students, the upgrade could reduce the gap between asking a question and actually understanding the mechanism behind it.

For the AI product landscape, the update also raises the bar for learning-focused assistants. Competing chat products now face pressure to provide not only accurate explanations but also visual and iterative teaching tools that support deeper comprehension.
